About Me

Data Science and Computational Biology Lab (University of Miami)

Jan 2024—Present

Student Researcher

I conduct research under Dr. Vanessa Aguiar-Pulido, leveraging computational tools to uncover molecular mechanisms underlying neurodevelopmental disorders. Currently, I'm analyzing single-cell RNA sequencing data from mouse models at three embryonic time points to study interneuron development and how genetic changes, like cD2 deletions, impact brain development. Using Python and the Scanpy framework, I perform preprocessing (quality control, normalization, and feature selection), dimensionality reduction (PCA, UMAP), and implement unsupervised machine learning methods (Leiden clustering) to identify cell populations and characterize shifts in cell-type composition. I also apply Scanorama for batch correction and use PAGA with Diffusion Pseudotime for pseudotime inference to map developmental trajectories across wild-type and knockout genotypes. Previously, I used R to explore overlaps between poison exons and minor intron genes, investigating their role in disorders such as Dravet Syndrome.
